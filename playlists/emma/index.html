Emma's Pandemic Playlist

As much that COVID-19 has destroyed the world in physical and psychological health, there also was a lot of moments that outshined the negative clouds corroding us. I believe everything happens for a reason, this virus has taught us to never take life, memories, family, or friendships for granted. My top ten songs are a lot - bops that made me sing my lungs out, lyrics making me remember the past, songs heard in moments I'll never forget, or words that reflect on the life to come after we get through this.


7 Summers by Morgan Wallen
S&M by Rihanna
Out West by JACKBOYS and Travis Scott (feat. Young Thug)
Stay in My Corner by The Arcs
You and Islands by Zac Brown Band
betty by Taylor Swift
Russian Roulette by Rihanna
Six Feet Apart by Luke Combs
Faith by The Weeknd
Holy by Justin Bieber (feat. Chance the Rapper)


The Sounds

The artists fall into a plethora of genres: country, r&b, hip-hop, and alternative. I grew up listening to country and my happiest childhood memories are the days my family would be driving to the beach blasting it while the sun shined and waves crashed. As I grew older, I got into hip-hop and r&b as a lot of young adults do, and this style of music pumps me up and makes me want to never start partying. Alternative is a source of both calmness and difference in vibes from my two opposing genres. In the pandemic, country played the biggest part in helping me reminisce my past as well as good times with my friends. 7 Summers was my top song, and not only do I absolutely adore Morgan Wallen, but the title reminded me of my childhood in the past summers, same as betty. S&M has always made me want to get up and dance, as well as Russian Roulette and Out West. Songs like You and Islands and Six Feet Apart are more pandemic based and inspire me to look forward to what is to come in the future. Holy and Faith are more religious as I am Catholic, but inspire hopefulness as well. Then, songs like Stay in My Corner played when I was carefree and went to the lake to escape from the madness and get into nature over Summer. All incredibly different but meaningful to me.
